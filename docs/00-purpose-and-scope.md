# Purpose and Scope

## Purpose

Pathogen genomics surveillance depends on more than sequencing capacity. Countries and laboratories also need durable systems for data quality, storage, backup, metadata, workflow execution, provenance, access control, analysis, reporting, and long-term stewardship.

This knowledge base consolidates pathogen-agnostic guidance that can be reused across disease programmes and adapted into formal guidance products.

## Intended Audiences

- National public health laboratories
- Veterinary and One Health laboratories
- Bioinformaticians and data managers
- Laboratory leadership and programme managers
- Ministry, funder, and procurement teams
- Central IT, cybersecurity, and information governance teams
- Technical working groups developing WHO, PHA4GE, CPG, CGPS, Fiocruz, or disease-programme guidance

## Scope

In scope:

- practical bioinformatics infrastructure principles
- data lifecycle management
- general QC and analytical principles
- storage, backup, archiving, and recovery
- workflow systems and reproducibility
- metadata, identifiers, and data standards
- access control, governance, and security
- implementation readiness, staffing, sustainability, and procurement

Out of scope unless referenced as examples:

- pathogen-specific wet-lab protocols
- pathogen-specific analytical interpretation thresholds
- clinical decision rules
- legal advice for a specific jurisdiction
- vendor endorsement

## Editorial Model

Each guidance module should be:

- pathogen-agnostic by default
- implementation-oriented
- explicit about assumptions and maturity level
- traceable to source material
- written so it can be copied into formal guidance with minimal rewriting
