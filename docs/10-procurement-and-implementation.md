# Procurement and Implementation

## Core Principle

Procurement for pathogen genomics infrastructure should be based on use cases, data flows, staffing, governance, sustainability, and required service levels. Buying compute or storage without an operating model creates long-term risk.

## Procurement Questions

- What surveillance workflows must the system support?
- What sample volume is expected now and in three years?
- What data must enter, leave, or remain inside the system?
- What legal, governance, or residency constraints apply?
- Who will operate and maintain the system?
- How will workflows be installed, validated, updated, and retired?
- What backup and disaster recovery requirements apply?
- What user roles and identity systems are required?
- What monitoring, logging, and support are needed?

## Implementation Sequence

1. Define use cases and workflows.
2. Map the data lifecycle.
3. Identify governance and access requirements.
4. Estimate throughput, storage, and compute needs.
5. Choose an operating model.
6. Select infrastructure and workflow tooling.
7. Pilot with real data.
8. Validate routine workflows.
9. Document support, backup, and incident processes.
10. Review after routine operation begins.
