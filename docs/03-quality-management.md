# Quality Management

## Purpose

Hold source-backed material on quality management across pathogen genomics data and analysis workflows.

## Source Basis

- PHA4GE workflow and discussion sections support transparency, workflow versioning, portability, and reproducibility.
- `knowledge-base/source-cards/phe-implementing-pathogen-genomics-case-study.md`
- Grant et al. 2018, `Implementing pathogen genomics: a case study`, Public Health England.

## Extracted/Adapted Text

The PHA4GE source supports the idea that routine analysis should be transparent, portable, reproducible, and auditable through workflow systems, containers, and provenance-aware infrastructure.

The PHE case study supports quality-management content for implementation: before switching off traditional testing, conventional and WGS workflows were run in parallel, datasets were compared, gap analysis was performed, protocols were adjusted, quality parameters were established across WGS typing steps, and input/output material quality was assessed. The case study also reports ISO 15189 accreditation for the central sequencing service and WGS reference-laboratory services.

## Implementation Notes

This is not yet a full quality-management chapter. Current source material supports validation, accreditation, and computational reproducibility, but does not yet define a generic quality-management system.
