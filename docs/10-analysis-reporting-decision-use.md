# Analysis, Reporting, and Decision Use

## Purpose

Hold source-backed material on how analyses and reports support public-health decision use.

## Extracted/Adapted Text

The current source material supports a limited point: pathogen genomic outputs can inform policy and regulation, and data flow includes reports, visualisations, and final analysis results. It also distinguishes intermediate outputs that support interpretation from final outputs that constitute conclusions generated from the data.

Armstrong et al. provide public-health use examples across foodborne outbreak investigation, tuberculosis control, influenza surveillance, antimicrobial-resistance surveillance, malaria surveillance, parasitic disease investigation, and molecular surveillance. The PHE case study provides operational reporting examples: a five-day sample-submission-to-final-report service, reports combining LIMS and sequence-analysis data, and outbreak examples where WGS helped refine case definitions, identify sources, and support interventions.
