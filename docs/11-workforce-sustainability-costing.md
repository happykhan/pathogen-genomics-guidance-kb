## Extracted/Adapted Text

The PHA4GE source states that infrastructure requires workflow execution engines, data management systems, system configuration, and human resources to maintain it. The vignettes illustrate different maintenance burdens across laptop, on-premises, cloud/PaaS, Galaxy, Terra, and IRIDA-like systems.

The survey dimensions include ease of use for administrators, ease of use for users, future proofing, flexibility, scalability, data provenance and management, access control, and external access requirements. These dimensions can inform sustainability discussion, but they are not a costing model.

The PHE case study supports implementation costing and workforce content through business-case development, capital planning, equipment and IT infrastructure costs, workforce/training review, specialised bioinformatics and computing/software development roles, user engagement, and reported operational impact including staff reallocation and reduced staff costs in the reference laboratory. Armstrong et al. support workforce development across microbiology, epidemiology, bioinformatics vocabulary, and data-science skills.

## Implementation Notes

The source supports workforce, training, maintainability, and one case-study capital-planning example, but does not define roles, staffing ratios, recurrent cost categories, procurement budgets, or total cost of ownership.
