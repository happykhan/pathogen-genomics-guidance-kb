## Extracted/Adapted Text

The PHA4GE source frames IAM as a continuous responsibility covering provisioning, role assignment, access review, offboarding, monitoring, and auditability. It states that pathogen genomics infrastructure often requires coordination with central IT, external providers, cybersecurity, and information governance.

The source also notes that responsibilities for detecting and responding to data breaches or abuse of compute resources should be defined, and that external services can shift some operational responsibility to providers.

## Implementation Notes

The source strongly supports IAM and incident-responsibility framing. It does not provide a complete ethics, privacy, legal, or benefit-sharing chapter.
