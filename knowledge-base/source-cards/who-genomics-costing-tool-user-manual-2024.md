## Extracted Notes

- The manual describes a Genomics Costing Tool designed for budgeting and resource mobilization for infrastructure, workforce, biosafety and quality assurance associated with genomic surveillance.
- Requested inputs include annual throughput, sequencing platform, personnel involved in sequencing and bioinformatics, salary and training costs, facility-related costs, sample transport costs, quality-management costs, and bioinformatics costs.
- The tool covers cloud computing, in-house server costs, hybrid bioinformatics arrangements, software licensing, storage, hardware, maintenance fees, and on-premises solutions.
- Cost outputs are organized by categories including reagents/consumables, equipment, personnel/training, facilities/transportation, bioinformatics, and quality management.
- The manual supports scenario comparison by throughput and platform, while noting limitations of the SARS-CoV-2-specific version and single-platform assumptions.

## Relevant Guide Sections

- Cost the recurrent service, not only the initial purchase
- Make the investment case with explicit assumptions
- Treat infrastructure as an operating model
- Separate active storage, backup, archive, and retention
- Plan workforce and training as core infrastructure
- Validate before replacing established methods

## Gaps

- Current tool version is SARS-CoV-2 focused and does not fully solve multi-pathogen or mixed-platform costing. It still provides a strong source-backed category model.
