## Status

Source-backed. High-priority source for national strategy, action-plan, lifecycle, metadata, data-management, sharing, and costed implementation guidance.

## Extracted Notes

- Frames genomic surveillance strategy around national context, priority pathogen use cases, readiness for novel pathogens, and integration across public-health surveillance systems.
- Presents a value-chain model that starts with priority use cases and connects sample/data collection, extraction, sequencing, bioinformatics analysis, public-health analysis, interpretation, data publication, and result sharing.
- States that genetic sequence data should be integrated with clinical, laboratory, epidemiological, One Health, phenotypic and other contextual data.
- Provides source-backed support for treating metadata quality, data quality, internal consistency, SOPs, internal/external quality assessment, analysis/interpretation capacity, privacy/security, public-health reporting, and data sharing as part of national genomic surveillance design.
- Supports a costed implementation plan where prioritized activities have responsible entities, implementation level, timeline, monitoring indicators, and cost, and where shared laboratory/bioinformatics costs are pooled across relevant national plans where appropriate.

## Relevant Guide Sections

- Why pathogen genomics?
- Start from the public-health decision
- Design the sample-to-report data lifecycle
- Integrate genomic, laboratory, and epidemiological data
- Plan data sharing as a governed decision
- Cost the recurrent service, not only the initial purchase
