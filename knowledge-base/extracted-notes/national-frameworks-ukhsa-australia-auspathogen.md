## Relevant Extracted Points

### UKHSA Strategy

- UKHSA frames its genomics strategy around integrating genomics into infectious-disease control and public-health decision-making.
- Implementation requirements include workforce, laboratories, data and analytics capabilities, and collaboration with NHS, academic, and industry partners.
- Strategic aims include linking pathogen genomic data to epidemiological data, incorporating genomic data into public-health systems, developing best-practice guidelines for data use, supporting clinical and public-health decision-making, and improving data sharing and transparency.
- UKHSA explicitly names scalable, robust and standardised laboratory services, bioinformatic analysis pipelines, translational services, workforce development, data sharing, global collaboration, sustainability, and value-for-money evaluation.

### Australian National Framework

- The Australian framework is directed at decision-makers, funders, and policymakers.
- It frames microbial genomics as a tool for surveillance, outbreak investigation, health service planning, public-health decision-making, and vaccine/treatment support.
- It identifies system needs including governance, leadership, financing, laboratory capability, human resources, information systems, access to essential medicines/technologies, and service delivery.
- Strategic priorities include a harmonised national approach, technology and data governance, integration into public health, access and workforce, and financing.
- The framework explicitly discusses nationally agreed data governance, ethical and equitable data sharing, metadata connection, data storage and infrastructure, interoperability, quality assurance, proficiency testing, and high-performance computing infrastructure for storage and sharing.

### AusPathoGen Implementation

- AusPathoGen is described as a national partnership between academia, public-health laboratories, and public-health agencies.
- Key enablers include inclusive collaboration, clear public-health priorities, data integration and analysis, AusTrakka, standard bioinformatic methods, ethics and data-sharing agreements, training public-health officials to interpret and act on genomic data, and evaluation/cost-effectiveness work.
- The programme used governance committees, pathogen-specific working groups, bioinformatics sub-working groups, a secure data-analysis platform, national ethics and data-sharing arrangements, and cross-jurisdictional collaboration.
- AusTrakka is presented as a secure platform for data sharing, integration, management, visualisation, and public-health surveillance use.
- The source emphasises training data generators and end users, evaluating public-health usefulness, and avoiding siloed implementation.
