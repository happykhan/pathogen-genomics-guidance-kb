## Relevant Extracted Points

### Surveillance Framing

- ECDC frames WGS as a way to strengthen outbreak investigations and public-health surveillance, not as a standalone laboratory technology.
- It recognises WGS as a reference microbial typing method in outbreak studies and an increasingly used method for national surveillance.
- It links the value of WGS to improved accuracy and effectiveness of disease surveillance, outbreak investigation, and evaluation of prevention policies.

### Genomic Epidemiology

- ECDC emphasises interpretation of genomic data together with epidemiological data.
- It calls for training that develops an integrative genomic epidemiology discipline across public-health microbiology, epidemiology, and risk management.

### Quality And Harmonisation

- Sequencing quality-control metrics and minimum quality-assurance standards should be agreed for each pathogen.
- ECDC identifies ring trials and external quality assessment as mechanisms for testing reproducibility across laboratories using different sequencing and bioinformatics platforms.
- It calls for harmonised analytical strategies, visualisation, and plain-language reporting for epidemiological inference and risk assessment.

### Data Linkage And Governance

- WGS records should be linked with patient, source, or environmental metadata using a common source identifier as part of surveillance notification.
- It distinguishes non-confidential minimum contextual data suitable for public databases from sensitive clinical and epidemiological data restricted to competent health authorities.
- It calls for formal governance of data sharing and use agreements for each disease surveillance scheme.

### Infrastructure And Investment

- Full implementation requires investment in sequencing instruments, validated bioinformatics tools, laboratory informatics infrastructure, operational funding, and competence development for epidemiologists and risk managers.
- It anticipates that case definitions, data sources, data flows, data processing, storage, and analysis plans may need to change as WGS is integrated into surveillance.
